## 2. Legal Framework for Foreign Buyers

### Property Ownership Rights

- **Foreigners have equal ownership rights** to Costa Rican citizens in most areas
- **No residency required** to purchase property
- Ownership type: **Fee Simple** (strongest form — buy, sell, lease, inherit)
- Many foreigners use **Sociedad Anónima (S.A.)** corporations for liability protection and tax benefits

### Maritime Zone Law (ZMT) — Critical Restriction

| Zone | Distance from High Tide | Rules |
|------|------------------------|-------|
| **Public Zone** | 0–50 meters | Cannot be privately owned or developed |
| **Concession Zone** | 50–200 meters | Lease only, not outright ownership |
| **Restricted Foreign Ownership** | Concession land | Foreigners limited to 49% unless 5+ years of residency |

> **Implication for website**: Properties near the coast need clear ZMT status indicators. Listing data should distinguish titled vs. concession land.

### Residency via Investment

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Minimum Investment** | $150,000 USD (reduced from $200K under Law 9996, 2021) |
| **Property Registration** | Must be under personal name, not corporation (as of 2026) |
| **Processing Time** | 6–8 months |
| **Duration** | 2-year temporary residency, renewable |
| **Path to Permanent** | After 3 consecutive years of temporary residency |
| **Citizenship** | 7+ years of legal residency |
| **Dependents** | Spouse and children can be included |

### Agent Licensing

- Costa Rica has **no national real estate licensing law**
- Agents handling money must register with **SUGEF** (financial regulator) — requires residency + background check
- Voluntary associations: **CRGAR** and **CCCBR** provide ethics training and certification
- **RE/MAX Costa Rica**: Internal "RE/MAX Costa Rica University" training; agents adhere to NAR Code of Ethics

## 3. Property Types & Classification

### Standard Types (from API + market)

| Type | Description | Target Buyer |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Houses/Villas** | Ocean-view, jungle retreats, gated communities | Retirees, families |
| **Condos/Apartments** | Low-maintenance, amenity-rich, urban/tourist areas | Digital nomads, snowbirds |
| **Land/Lots** | Raw land for custom builds, farming, eco-projects | Investors, builders |
| **Commercial** | Hotels, restaurants, shops in tourist corridors | Business investors |
| **Farms/Fincas** | Agricultural land, often with existing operations | Lifestyle/agricultural investors |
| **Rentals** | Long-term and vacation rentals (furnished/unfurnished) | Digital nomads, snowbirds, tourists |

### Special Land Classifications

These are critical distinctions the website must surface clearly — especially for the Dominical/Uvita coastal market:

| Classification | Description | Key Rules |
|---------------|------------|-----------|
| **Titled Land (Fee Simple)** | Standard ownership recorded in National Registry | Full ownership rights; foreigners can buy freely |
| **ZMT / Concession Land** | Within 50–200m of high tide; governed by Maritime Zone Law | Lease-only (not owned); foreigners limited to 49% unless 5+ years residency; municipality grants concessions |
| **Conservation Land** | Protected areas under SINAC (National System of Conservation Areas) | Cannot be developed; includes national parks, biological reserves, wildlife refuges, and buffer zones; some adjacent private land has building restrictions |
| **Indigenous Reserves** | Territories designated for indigenous communities | Cannot be purchased by non-indigenous individuals |
| **Agricultural Use (Uso Agropecuario)** | Zoned exclusively for farming/ranching | Restrictions on residential or commercial development; may require IDA/INDER authorization for subdivision |

> **Implication for website**: Every listing should clearly display its land classification. ZMT concession properties and conservation-adjacent land require specific disclaimers and buyer education. This is a major trust differentiator — most competitor sites don't surface this information.

### Rental Market

The rental segment is particularly relevant for RE/MAX Altitud's coverage areas:

| Rental Type | Description | Typical Market |
|------------|------------|---------------|
| **Vacation Rentals** | Short-term (nightly/weekly), usually furnished with amenities | Tourists, Airbnb/VRBO market in Dominical/Uvita |
| **Long-term Rentals** | 6–12+ month leases, furnished or unfurnished | Expats, digital nomads, relocating families |
| **Property Management** | Full-service management for absentee owners | Investors who rent while abroad |

- **Rental income potential**: 2BR ocean-view properties in Costa Ballena generate **$40K–$80K/year**
- **Occupancy rates** are steadily increasing with tourism growth
- Costa Rica requires rental income to be reported; a **15% withholding tax** applies to non-resident landlords
- **ICT (Tourism Board) registration** required for short-term vacation rentals
- Rental listings attract a different search intent — drives traffic from "rent in Costa Rica" keywords

### Zoning System

- Each of Costa Rica's **84 municipalities** has a **Plan Regulador** (regulatory plan)
- Dictates: land use, building density, height restrictions, urban development rules
- Buyers should obtain **Certificación de Uso de Suelo** from municipality
- Urban zones have stricter regulations than rural ones

## 4. Transaction Process & Costs

### Timeline Overview

```
Property Search  →  Offer/LOI  →  SPA Agreement  →  Escrow Deposit  →  Due Diligence  →  Closing  →  Registration
   weeks–months     1–3 days       3–7 days          concurrent         30–45 days       5 bus. days   7–10 days
```

### Closing Costs (3–5% of purchase price)

| Component | Cost | Paid By |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Transfer Tax** | 1.5% | Buyer (negotiable) |
| **Registry/Documentary Stamps** | 0.5–0.8% | Buyer |
| **Notary/Legal Fees** | 1–2% + 13% VAT | Buyer |
| **Escrow Fees** | Variable + 13% VAT | Buyer |
| **Real Estate Commission** | 5–6% + 13% VAT | Seller |
| **Annual Property Tax** | ~0.25% of registered value | Owner |

### Due Diligence Checklist

- ✅ Title search at National Registry (Folio Real)
- ✅ Cadastral survey (Plano Catastrado) verification
- ✅ Encumbrance check (mortgages, liens, easements)
- ✅ Corporate ownership verification (if applicable)
- ✅ Water availability (AyA/ASADA letters)
- ✅ Zoning compliance (Uso de Suelo certificate)
- ✅ Building permits and environmental regulations
- ✅ Physical property inspection
- ✅ Tax payment status verification

> **Implication for website**: Due diligence checklists and buying guides are high-value content for SEO and user trust.

## 6. Listing & MLS Ecosystem

### Critical Insight: No Centralized MLS

Costa Rica **does not have a unified national MLS** like the US. This creates both challenges and opportunities:

| Factor | Impact on RE/MAX Altitud |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Fragmented listings** | RE/MAX's own API data becomes a competitive advantage |
| **No Zillow/Redfin** | No dominant aggregator for CR — opportunity to own SEO |
| **Agent networks** | Rely on personal networks, WhatsApp groups for sharing |
| **Encuentra24** | Dominant classifieds — but generic, not luxury/curated |
| **CostaRicaMLS** | Attempting structure, but limited coverage |
| **OmniMLS (2023)** | Connecting agents internationally, but incomplete |

> **Strategic opportunity**: A well-built, SEO-optimized RE/MAX Altitud site with daily-synced API data can become the authoritative source for Pérez Zeledón and Southern Pacific listings — filling the void left by the absence of a centralized MLS.
